# Chapter 4: Understanding the Prospect and Resident Journey in Multifamily

## 4.1 Prospect in Multifamily

In the multifamily leasing context, prospects refer to potential renters actively searching for a new place to live. These individuals are exploring different housing options, evaluating properties, and engaging with leasing teams as they move toward a leasing decision.

---

**4.1.1 Stages of the Prospect Journey in Multifamily Leasing**

The prospect journey in multifamily leasing consists of several key stages, from the initial apartment search to the final lease signing. Understanding each stage helps leasing teams tailor their communication strategies to better guide prospects toward becoming residents.

https://res.cloudinary.com/dtlycu2wl/video/upload/v1780411400/Multifamily_Leasing_Journey_ebgldq.mp4

## Key Takeaways

- The multifamily prospect journey typically includes 6 stages — from apartment search to lease signing.
- Most prospects begin their search through ILS platforms, Google, social media, and property websites.
- Guest cards help leasing teams start communication, follow-ups, and tour coordination with prospects.
- PMCs now use tools like Hayley AAI to automate tour scheduling, reminders, follow-ups, and prospect communication across voice, mail, and chat.
- The touring experience plays a major role in leasing decisions by helping prospects evaluate the property, amenities, and community atmosphere.
- Clear communication during applications and lease signing helps reduce friction and improve the resident onboarding experience.

---

**4.1.2 Key Pain Points for Prospects During Property Searches**

Prospects face several challenges when searching for multifamily properties, which can make the process feel overwhelming and time-consuming. Understanding these pain points helps leasing teams build better experiences — and gives operators a clear picture of where friction costs them leases.

https://res.cloudinary.com/dtlycu2wl/video/upload/v1780411539/Property_Search_Journey_pznyge.mp4

## Key Takeaways

- Prospects often struggle with outdated listings, inconsistent information, and too many apartment options across platforms.
- Flexible touring options like virtual and self-guided tours have become increasingly important for modern prospects.
- Hidden fees, complex applications, and delayed responses can negatively impact leasing decisions and prospect trust.
- PMCs now use tools like Hayley AAI to improve communication, automate scheduling and follow-ups, and create a smoother leasing experience across voice, mail, and chat.
- Faster and more consistent communication is now one of the biggest drivers of leasing conversion and prospect satisfaction.

### Knowledge Check:

**Question:** A prospect compares listings, pricing, amenities, reviews, and floor plans online. Which journey stage are they in?

A. A renewal decision after living onsite

B. An apartment search before choosing properties

C. A move-in process after lease signing

D. A resident engagement phase after settling in

**Correct Answer:** B — Apartment search is correct because prospects begin by comparing communities, prices, amenities, reviews, and available floor plans.

## 4.2 Resident in Multifamily

Residents in multifamily housing represent a diverse group of individuals and families who rent apartments or units within a community. Their journey from pre-move-in to lease renewal or move-out involves several stages, each offering meaningful opportunities to foster positive experiences and build lasting satisfaction.

---

**4.2.1 Stages of the Resident Journey**

The resident journey in multifamily housing consists of several key stages. Each stage offers opportunities for property managers to engage residents and ensure their experience is positive, ultimately influencing satisfaction and retention rates.

**4.2.2 Key Pain Points for Residents in Multifamily Housing**

Residents in multifamily housing face a variety of challenges that can negatively impact their experience and satisfaction. ### Knowledge Check:

**Question:** Why are the first 30 to 90 days important in the resident journey?

A. They only affect move-out paperwork later

B. They shape early satisfaction and retention
