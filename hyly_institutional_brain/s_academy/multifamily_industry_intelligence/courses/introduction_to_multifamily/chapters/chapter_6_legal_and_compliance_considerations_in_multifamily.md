## 6.2 Fair Housing Act and Its Impact on Multifamily Operations

The Fair Housing Act (FHA) has a profound impact on multifamily property operations by prohibiting discrimination in housing-related activities. Property managers must ensure they comply with the FHA in all aspects of managing residents, from leasing to ongoing services.

https://res.cloudinary.com/dtlycu2wl/video/upload/v1780413345/Fair_Housing_Act_Classes_sh2ucf.mp4

## Key Takeaways

- The Fair Housing Act protects individuals from housing discrimination based on seven federally protected classes.
- Fair and consistent leasing, screening, and communication practices are essential in multifamily housing.
- Property teams must support accessibility and reasonable accommodations for residents with disabilities.
- State and local housing laws may include additional protected classes beyond federal requirements.

## 6.3 Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) Implications for Multifamily Properties

https://res.cloudinary.com/dtlycu2wl/video/upload/v1780413248/ADA_Multifamily_Implications_kxnafw.mp4

## Key Takeaways

- Multifamily properties must ensure accessibility in shared public spaces such as leasing offices and amenities.
- Property managers are required to provide reasonable accommodations for residents with disabilities when needed.
- Accessibility standards apply during property construction, renovations, and upgrades to common areas.
- Communication methods, websites, and resident documents should remain accessible to all individuals.
- Understanding ADA responsibilities helps create more inclusive communities and reduces compliance risks.

## 6.4 The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and Multifamily Property Management

https://res.cloudinary.com/dtlycu2wl/video/upload/v1780413108/Digital_Compliance_Evolution_cavibi.mp4

### Key Takeaways

1. Multifamily properties must clearly disclose how resident and prospect data is collected and used.
2. California residents have privacy rights, including access, correction, deletion, and opt-out requests for personal information.
3. Properties using digital leasing, CRM systems, or smart-home technology may fall under CCPA compliance requirements.
4. Secure data management and proper privacy policies help reduce legal and operational risk.
5. Non-compliance with CCPA regulations can result in financial penalties and loss of resident trust.
