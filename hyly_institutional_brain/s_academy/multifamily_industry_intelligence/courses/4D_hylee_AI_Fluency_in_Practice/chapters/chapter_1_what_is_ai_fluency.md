## 1.2 The 4 E’s of AI Fluency

AI fluency is different from AI literacy. AI literacy means knowing that AI exists or knowing how to open an AI tool. AI fluency means being able to work with AI confidently and naturally, in a way that improves real work.

**AI Fluency = The ability to work with AI in a way that is Effective, Efficient, Ethical, and Evidenced.**

### The Solution — The 4 E’s

https://res.cloudinary.com/dtlycu2wl/image/upload/v1776930673/ch1_4es_ai_fluency_1_ogneos.png

| E | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Effective** | AI helps solve the right problem and improves the quality of the work. |
| **Efficient** | AI saves time, reduces rework, or speeds up execution. |
| **Ethical** | AI is used responsibly, with care for data, context, and impact. |
| **Evidenced** | AI output is checked, supported, and not accepted blindly. |

### Key Takeaways

- AI fluency is not the same as AI literacy.
- AI fluency is measured by outcomes, not tool count.
- Fast output is not enough.
- Fluent AI use must be useful, responsible, and checked.
- You need a framework before relying on AI in real work.

## 1.3 The 4D Framework

| D | Core Question | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| **Delegation** | “What should I do vs. what should AI do?” | Deciding when to use AI, which tool to use, and what to keep human. |
| **Description** | “How do I talk to AI so it actually helps?” | Communicating with AI clearly — not just prompting, but conversing. |
| **Discernment** | “Is what AI gave me actually good?” | Evaluating output for accuracy, completeness, and context-fit. |
| **Diligence** | “Am I using AI responsibly?” | Protecting data, checking facts, and owning what you produce. |

> Fluency is not about memorizing perfect prompts or knowing every AI tool. It is about thinking and working with AI naturally — like a second language.
