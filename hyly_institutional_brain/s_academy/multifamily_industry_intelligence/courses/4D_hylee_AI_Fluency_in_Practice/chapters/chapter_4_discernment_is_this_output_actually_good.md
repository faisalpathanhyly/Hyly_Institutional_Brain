# Chapter 4: Discernment — Is This Output Actually Good?

This chapter introduces the third D: **Discernment**. Once AI gives an output, you should not accept it blindly.

## 4.1 What Discernment Means

### Watch: Discernment

https://res.cloudinary.com/dtlycu2wl/video/upload/v1777011128/Chapter_4__Discernment_ucvuae.mp4

**Discernment = Evaluating AI output to check if it is accurate, complete, and actually useful for your specific situation — before you act on it.**

### Knowledge Check

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| What does “Confident ≠ Correct” mean? | AI output can sound polished and certain, but still be inaccurate, incomplete, or wrong for the situation. |

## 4.2 The 3 Levels of Discernment

https://res.cloudinary.com/dtlycu2wl/image/upload/v1776941108/ch4_discernment_levels_ujjyrq.png

| Level | What You Check | Key Question |
|---|---|---|
| **Level 1 — Product Discernment** | Is the output itself correct? | Is it accurate, complete, and relevant to my actual situation? |
| **Level 2 — Process Discernment** | Did AI reason correctly? | Did it make the right assumptions? Did it consider all important factors? |
| **Level 3 — Performance Discernment** | Did AI collaborate well? | Did it stay in the right role, use the right detail level, and actually help? |

### Knowledge Check

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| What are the three levels of Discernment? | Product Discernment, Process Discernment, and Performance Discernment. |

## 4.3 The Description ↔ Discernment Loop

https://res.cloudinary.com/dtlycu2wl/image/upload/v1776941109/ch4_refine_loop_1_k31cmt.png

1. Describe the task
2. Receive AI output
3. Review the output using Discernment
4. Identify what was missing, wrong, unclear, or misaligned
5. Improve the next prompt
6. Repeat until the output is useful

### Knowledge Check

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| Why is the second prompt often better than the first? | Because the learner uses what they noticed in the first output to improve the next Description. |

## 4.4 Discernment in Hyly Workflows

| Scenario | What Looked Right | What Was Actually Wrong | Who Catches It |
|---|---|---|---|
| **P0 Bug Classification** — QA automation flags a bug as Critical | Clean label, correct field mapping, proper ownership assigned | May not match actual business severity — could be a minor visual glitch, not production-breaking | Product Manager |
| **“Better Reporting” Request** — Client request enters the pipeline | Clear, actionable-looking feature request | Could be a training gap — client has not configured their dashboard — not a product gap | Business Owners |
