# Chapter 1: Introduction to the Multifamily Industry

## 1.1 Introduction to the Multifamily Industry

Chapter 1 is your foundation. By the end, you'll have a solid understanding of the following:

- What the multifamily industry is and how it operates
- How multifamily properties are classified and managed
- The major market segments shaping the industry today
- The key organizations influencing multifamily housing
- Where technology and AI fit into modern multifamily operations
- The industry language and context are needed to speak confidently with clients
- Foundational concepts that help both new hires and experienced CSMs better understand the world their clients operate in

https://res.cloudinary.com/dtlycu2wl/video/upload/v1779292674/Multifamily_Foundations_f8i4lv.mp4

**Key Takeaways**

- Multifamily housing serves **~40 million Americans** across property types ranging from high-rises to single-family rentals
- The industry contributes **$3.4 trillion** to the U.S. economy and supports **17.5 million jobs**
- Nearly **half of all U.S. renters** — 22.7 million households — are cost-burdened, spending more than 30% of their income on housing; this is what keeps rental demand consistently strong
- As of 2025, **68% of multifamily operators** have integrated AI into their core operations — technology is now a standard part of how the industry runs, not an emerging trend.

**Key Takeaways**

- Well-run multifamily properties balance **resident experience** and **operational efficiency** — the 6 characteristics in this section are where those two things meet
- **AI-powered operations** are now a standard characteristic — as of 2025, 77% of operators using AI report reduced operating expenses and 85% report improved lead-to-lease conversion rates

**Key Takeaways**

- The multifamily lifecycle has **6 phases** — each with its own focus, timeline, and stakeholders; understanding these helps you speak to where a client's property is and what they are prioritizing
- On average it takes **19.6 months** to complete a multifamily building — up from earlier estimates as construction complexity and costs have grown
- **Stabilized operations** is no longer just about filling units — in 2025, **57% of all leasing activity is renewals**, meaning resident retention is just as critical as new lease acquisition
- Office-to-apartment conversions hit a **record 70,700 units in the 2025 pipeline** — a growing alternative to ground-up development reshaping how new supply enters the market

## 1.4 Major Market Segments & Organizations Shaping the Multifamily

Multifamily housing serves different renter needs, lifestyles, and income levels. Because of this, the industry is divided into several market segments — each with its own audience, amenities, pricing, and operational focus.

The industry is also shaped by organizations including owners, operators, associations, and research groups that influence:

- Industry standards and best practices
- Investment and operational strategies
- Resident experience and property performance

### 1.4.1 Major Market Segments in Multifamily

https://res.cloudinary.com/dtlycu2wl/image/upload/v1779293992/market_segment_tfwy9w.webp

### 1.4.2 Organizations Shaping the Multifamily Industry

https://res.cloudinary.com/dtlycu2wl/video/upload/v1779294023/8_Orgs_Shaping_Multifamily_npxywv.mp4

**Key Takeaways**

- Multifamily is shaped by both **market segments** and the organizations that influence how communities are owned, managed, built, and evaluated
- Major market segments help operators understand **who they serve**, what residents expect, and how each property type competes
- Industry associations like **NMHC (National Multifamily Housing Council) and NAA (National Apartment Association)** provide data, advocacy, education, and standards that guide how the industry evolves
- Ownership groups such as **REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts)** influence investment strategies and large-scale portfolio decisions across markets
- **Property Management Companies (PMCs)** drive day-to-day operations — including leasing, marketing, and resident experience
- Research and certification bodies like **J Turner, IREM (Institute of Real Estate Management), and LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design)** influence how performance, reputation, professionalism, and sustainability are measured
